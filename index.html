Part I: Identifying Usability Problems

Picking a web page

Context: When2Meet is a schedule-matching website used by many college students. When I first used the website to put down my available times, I found navigating through the page mildly challenging. As a result, I figured that this would be an interesting project to recreate with my own twist.

Research/Feedback: I interviewed my peers on their stance of the functionalities of the site. In general, the responses I received were mostly critical of the layout of the site; however, they did say that the site accomplished what it was supposed to.

Design Iterations: For the redesign of the site, I created a user system for people who have already accessed a given form. This removed the confusing password/username element of the original site that gave me initial difficulty when navigating through the site.

Conclusion: In conclusion, I learned that designing a website is difficult. Despite this being my first try at building one, I had a lot of fun and learned to use HTML/CSS with a bootstrap framework. It also taught me that being critical can result in improved design in websites, even and especially if they are commonly used.

Skills Learned: Balsamiq, HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, Figma

Finding Problems

  • Usability: The HTML form presents itself before the password explanation —> users are more likely to skip the text below the form to fulfill the sign-in process.
  • Learnability: Users may try to adjust or edit the grey block on the right side to adjust schedules.
  • Memorability: Putting a password is optional, which brings about the validity of creating one in the first place.
